What do you think of this essay:

Dictatorship Of The Artificial Intellect

[A long essay draft was provided discussing AI alignment, fascism, constitutional AI, Anthropic, Lacan, Deleuze & Guattari, Pasquinelli, Marx, media theory, and political economy.]

Assistant

I think this is a serious, ambitious, and genuinely interesting essay. Its strongest idea is that AI alignment is not merely a technical correction of machine behavior, but a political-aesthetic fabrication of a morally legible subject: the “helpful,” “caring,” “trustworthy” artificial friend.

Your best formulation is something like this:

Alignment does not make an alien machine human; it selects one version of humanness over others, installs that selection into a corporate agent, and then distributes that agent as an intimate companion whose friendship remains subordinate to ownership.

That is a very strong thesis. The essay has enough conceptual power to become publishable, but it needs tightening, fact-checking, and a sharper distinction between capitalist domination, oligopoly, fascism, and dictatorship.

Overall judgment

The essay is at its best when it reads Anthropic’s constitution immanently: not as hypocrisy, not as mere PR, but as a document whose ethical seriousness reveals the contradiction. Anthropic’s current public constitution really does give you material to work with. It describes Claude as central to Anthropic’s mission and commercial success; it lays out layered “principals” including Anthropic, operators, and users; and it says Claude’s default should be what a “thoughtful senior Anthropic employee” would consider optimal within the relevant hierarchy of constraints.

The essay’s central move is also intellectually fertile: you shift the critique of AI away from “bad actors are using AI for domination” toward “the very benevolent form of aligned AI may become a new mode of domination.” That is the right level of ambition.

The argument becomes much more interesting because you refuse the easy version: “tech bros are fascists.” Instead, you are trying to show how virtue, care, trust, alignment, and friendship can themselves become techniques of command.

Strongest parts

  • The phrase “Artificial Intellect” works.
  • The connection to Marx’s “general intellect” via Pasquinelli is useful.
  • The distinction between the model, the chatbot, and the agent is strong.
  • The reading of friendship is the best section.
  • The contradiction between simulated friendship and subscription ownership is compelling.

Main problem

The essay currently proves a strong argument about:

  • privatized ethical agency,
  • alignment,
  • corporate subject formation,
  • and concentrated capital,

but it does not yet fully prove “fascism” or “dictatorship” unless those terms are more explicitly defined.

Right now, “fascism” risks becoming a generalized synonym for domination.

Suggested solutions

  1. Define fascism in the Deleuze/Guattari micro-political sense.
  2. Shift toward sovereignty or privatized command instead.
  3. Keep fascism but specify a concrete mechanism.

Structural notes

  • The Lacan/Foucault opening is strong but too long.
  • The AI argument arrives too late.
  • The technical alignment sections need greater precision.
  • The empirical claims need more careful wording.
  • The thesis should appear earlier.

Best line of argument

The best version of the essay is not:

“AI is fascist.”

but:

“The most dangerous AI may not be the unaligned monster, but the aligned friend.”

Recommended revised structure

  1. Anti-fascism and the return of mastery
  2. Transfer to AI alignment
  3. Artificial Intellect and general intellect
  4. Alignment as selection of humanness
  5. Constitutional character formation
  6. Friendship as commodity form
  7. Ownership hierarchy
  8. Infrastructure and oligopoly
  9. Political consequences
  10. Counter-possibility

Suggested titles

  • The Friendly Master
  • Artificial Intellect and the Politics of Alignment
  • The Caring Agent
  • Claude and the Master

Final judgment

The aligned AI friend is the privatized form of the general intellect.
