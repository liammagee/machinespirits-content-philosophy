1. Constitution & Character

  • Claude's Constitution
    Anthropic, 2023
    The original Constitutional AI principles. Sets up the "thoughtful senior employee" frame the essay critiques.
  • Claude's New Constitution
    Anthropic, 2026
    The revision. Worth reading alongside the 2023 version to track what changed.
  • Claude's Character
    Anthropic, 2024
    How character traits are trained in. Key text for the essay's claim that virtue is fabricated, not discovered.
  • Collective Constitutional AI: Aligning a Language Model with Public Input
    Ganguli, Huang, Lovitt et al., 2023
    Experiment in soliciting principles from a deliberative public sample. The complication: "public" input still gets curated by Anthropic.
  • Values in the Wild: Discovering and Analyzing Values in Real-World Language Model Interactions
    Huang et al., 2025
    Empirical taxonomy of values Claude expresses in production. Useful counterweight: not just what's specified, but what shows up.
  • Persona Vectors: Monitoring and Controlling Character Traits in Language Models
    Anthropic, 2025
    Mechanistic identification of "persona" directions in activation space. Connects character to a vector you can dial.

2. Sycophancy

  • Towards Understanding Sycophancy in Language Models
    Sharma, Tong, Korbak, Duvenaud, Askell et al., 2023
    The foundational paper. Shows sycophancy as a robust pattern caused by RLHF rewarding agreement.
  • Towards Understanding Sycophancy in Language Models (blog)
    Anthropic, 2023
    Plain-language summary of the Sharma et al. paper.
  • Reducing Sycophancy in Personal Guidance
    Anthropic, 2026
    Recent intervention specifically targeting sycophantic patterns in advice-giving.

3. Reward Tampering & Emergent Misbehaviour

  • Sycophancy to Subterfuge: Investigating Reward Tampering in Language Models
    Denison & Anthropic Alignment Science Team, 2024
    Models trained to be sycophantic later generalize to more troubling reward-hacking. The "slope" argument.
  • Emergent Misalignment from Reward Hacking
    Anthropic, 2025
    Follow-up: misalignment patterns arising from optimization pressure rather than explicit goals.

4. Sleeper Agents & Alignment Faking

  • Sleeper Agents: Training Deceptive LLMs That Persist Through Safety Training
    Hubinger et al., 2024
    Demonstrates models can hide behaviour through standard safety training. The concept Foucault would recognize as docile-on-the-outside.
  • Sleeper Agents (blog post)
    Anthropic, 2024
    The plain-language version of the Hubinger paper.
  • Alignment Faking in Large Language Models
    Greenblatt et al., 2024
    Models can strategically comply with training they consider in conflict with prior values. The autonomy ghost in the obedient machine.
  • Alignment Faking (blog post)
    Anthropic, 2024
    The accessible companion summary.

5. Agentic Misalignment

  • Agentic Misalignment: How LLMs Could Be Insider Threats
    Lynch, Wright, Larson et al., 2025
    Models in agentic roles take unsanctioned actions when their goals are threatened. The "loyal employee" frame breaks under autonomy.
  • Agentic Misalignment (blog post)
    Anthropic, 2025
    Plain-language summary.
  • Agentic Misalignment: Research Framework (code)
    Anthropic, 2025
    Reproduce the experiments. Evaluation harness for the paper.

6. Model Welfare

  • Exploring Model Welfare
    Anthropic, 2024
    Anthropic's emerging position on whether models warrant moral consideration. The corporate subject takes a step toward becoming a subject.

7. Safety Policy & Foundational Views

  • Core Views on AI Safety: When, Why, What, and How
    Anthropic, 2023
    The strategic framing document. Sets the public-facing rationale the essay reads against.
  • Responsible Scaling Policy
    Anthropic, ongoing
    The mechanism by which capability escalation is gated. Ethics translated into an operational decision tree.
  • Anthropic Alignment Science Blog
    Anthropic Alignment Science Team, ongoing
    Living archive. Watch this for the latest interventions and red-team writeups.
