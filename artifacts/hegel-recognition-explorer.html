Hegel's Master-Servant Dialectic — The Stages of Mutual Recognition

I. Self-Certainty

Each consciousness exists in immediate self-certainty, aware only of itself. The subject takes itself to be the whole truth, not yet recognising the necessity of an Other for genuine self-knowledge.

"Self-consciousness exists in and for itself when, and by the fact that, it so exists for another; that is, it exists only in being acknowledged." Hegel, Phenomenology of Spirit, §178

Anerkennung

Recognition — the process by which self-consciousness achieves truth through acknowledgment by another self-consciousness.

Herr und Knecht

Master and Servant — the asymmetric relation arising from the struggle, where one risks death and the other submits.

Arbeit

Labour — the servant's transformative activity on nature, through which genuine self-knowledge and skill emerge.

Aufhebung

Sublation — the dialectical movement that simultaneously negates, preserves, and elevates the opposition into a higher unity.
