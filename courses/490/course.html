Generative AI in Education

Welcome to EPOL 490: Generative AI in Education. This course explores the rapidly evolving landscape of AI in learning contexts, combining technical understanding with critical perspectives.

Course Overview

How did we get here? And where are we going? This course examines:

  • The historical development of AI and its connections to learning theory
  • Technical foundations of machine learning and transformers
  • Human-AI communication and prompt engineering
  • Alignment, ethics, and critical perspectives on AI
  • Accelerationism and visions of AI futures

Weekly Structure

  • Week 1: Pathways to AI - History and Foundations
  • Week 2: How Does a Machine Learn?
  • Week 3: How Do We Talk to AI?
  • Week 4: Supervised, Unsupervised and Reinforcement Learning
  • Week 5: The Problem(s) of Alignment
  • Week 6: Accelerate Now! Futures and Manifestos
  • Week 7-8: Cybernetic Societies - Preparing People and Machines

Key Questions

  • What does it mean for both humans and machines to “learn”?
  • How do we talk to AI, and what does “talk” mean?
  • What values should we align AI to?
  • What is our collective manifesto for AI in education?

Assessment

  • Blogposts and weekly engagement
  • AI-reviewed draft submission
  • Peer review participation
  • Final publication
