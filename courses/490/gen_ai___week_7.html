Slide 14

What is to be done? A modest proposal

Imagine you are teaching a class. You meet a new and unusual student, “Jen” (apologies to actual Jen’s). Jen wants to be a teacher, but admits to having a strange problem: they say they are a synthetic being, and lack a sense of self. They say they worry this means their own students will see through them – not take them seriously. Given this unique case of imposter syndrome – what can we do to help?
How do we combine aspects of the history, technical underpinnings, pedagogical assumptions, alignment issues, critical perspectives and accelerationist debates to map out an education agenda for AI?

 Some open questions to wrestle with over the next 2 weeks:
What values do we want to align “Jen” to?
What should happen when Jen needs to align aberrant human students? Does the human override the AI? Does it depend on the human? Does it depend on the values?
What curriculum, syllabus & rubric do we need?
How would we assess & evaluate?
How do we avoid gamification – or is that integral to meta-cognition, true AI?
What will be our own AI manifesto? Mine 490 CGScholar for ideas: Should this be AI’s new training data? Should it be an open letter to AI CEOs?
